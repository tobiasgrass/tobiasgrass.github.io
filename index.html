About me.

I am a theoretical physicist interested in quantum phenomena and condensed matter physics.

Currently, I work as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Joint Quantum Institute at the University of Maryland in College Park.

My research focuses on the control of quantum systems. The goal is to simulate complex many-body behavior, to realize exotic effects, or to harness quantum phenomena for real-world applications.

My favorite systems are cold atoms and trapped ions -- due to their immense flexibility. Using light-matter interactions, quantum engineering can also be achieved in solid state devices. In this context, I am studying optical control schemes applied to graphene in the quantum Hall regime.
